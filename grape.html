University Prepares To Offset The Costs Of A New Collective Agreement With Teaching Assistants

12/1/2017—According to a source close to the administration, the President is not concerned with the outcome of the GTA bargaining. The source explained, We [the university - editor's note] will just recalculate the graduate research scholarship. When we pointed out that the current collective agreement has earnings protection, the source doubted that such protection would be effective. Failing to hide his excitement, the source suggested that university has many ways to control funding, including offering a lower funding package to newcomers. Even if our bargaining team yields to GTAs' wage demands, the administration will just raise tuition fees to cover the costs of the new collective agreement, concluded the source, grinning. When we asked if the university considered the possibility of GTAs going on strike in response to such treatment, the source beamed with pride, That's why we are delaying bargaining - who will stay on a picket line in February?

Best Student Experience

11/24/2017—To live up to its ambitious slogan – “Best Student Experience” – Western University developed the Institutional Quality Assurance Process (IQAP) in 2011. According to Western's Quality Council, Western is committed to offering graduate and undergraduate programs of high quality.
Our quality assurance processes reflect our commitment to excellence in undergraduate and graduate education, reads Western's Institutional Quality Assurance Process policy document.
When asked about what's involved when a department undergoes an IQAP review, one graduate chair remarked that it involved careful strategy.
You need to ensure the IQAP review committee doesn't get to chatting with any Year X, mature, or non- externally funded students in the department. Smoke and mirrors until they're out the door.
A graduate student from the same department and the Chair of the Department's Graduate Association remarked that departments always manage to find money in the budget for IQAP reviews.
You should have seen the spread at the department's cocktail reception for the IQAP committee. Now it makes sense why our travel funding was cut back this year. We were very happy to sacrifice our funding to ensure the department received a high quality approval rating.
An anonymous source from SGPS stated that administration spends most of its budget on refreshments. Graduate students love free food. When administration know something bad is coming down the line, they pull out all the stops. Food Services makes a killing off graduate student money. With bargaining just around the corner, be sure to keep an eye out for these enticing spreads.

Provost: Increasing Tuition Is Not Enough To Maintain The University's Spending Pattern

11/15/2017—With government freezing the funding of universities, the provost lamented that regulation caps tuition increases at 3%, but was happy to report there is no such cap for international students. Whether 3% or more, she assured the campus community that tuition increases are a valuable tool. We have benefited a lot from the compliance of Western students with respect to tuition increases, provost reminded that while the tuition increase in Quebec resulted in student demonstrations, in this part of Ontario it went well. Still, she noted that the 3% tuition increase would only generate 1.5% of operational budget increase and that in recent years Western's expenditures increase 4.5% per year. Everyone will feel the cuts in spending, the provost concluded. When asked about the impact on the graduate students, the provost assured that the minimal guarantee for Doctoral students - which with the current level of fees leaves $872/month for international and $932/month for domestic students - remains intact. No comments were made with regard to Master's. — Western's Budget Town Hall, 31 Oct, UCC McKellar Room.

University Prepared For Bargaining With GTAs

10/2/2017—Ahead of this falls bargaining, Western's administration is confident. We don't need much preparation. We just offer them the same wage increase that we offer other campus unions says a member of the university's bargaining team, who asked to remain anonymous.

When asked for the rationale behind treating GTAs pay raises the same as pay raises to the much higher paid faculty and staff, the source explained, We find that strategy really removes the headache from the process. Otherwise we have to listen to them present these really sad cases of graduate students with children or health conditions which really bring us down. It's much better to not have to think about these things, and the simple 1-2% rule lets us just zone out for the bargaining meetings.

When asked if GTAs will accept this, the source explained That's the easiest part. We take something away right before bargaining. By the time they bargain for it back, they feel they gained something. When we asked a GTA to comment, his jaw dropped. Similar responses, apparently out of shock, mean we haven't yet got a GTA to comment verbally by the time of publishing.

Western Ensures That Graduate Students Have Stable Funding

9/25/2017—According to SGPS website,
While at Western, you will want to devote your full energy to the successful completion of your studies, so we want to ensure that stable funding is available to you.
We wanted to find out more, so we asked Abbas, a 4th year PhD student in Computer Science. Abbas came to Western to specialize in computing limit points of quasi-components of regular chains (and applications). When asked how he found the funding here, he appeared agitated: The minimum funding is 30,501 a year, but with tuition of $7026 for the fall term, I am left with $884 a month. Not only can I not afford to live anywhere near the campus, it is still not possible to pay for basic expenses like cell phone and groceries.

When we pointed out that Western ensures stable funding, not funding you can live on he appeared perplexed, This is my last year of funding. Like others in my program, I will need to be here for a fifth year. I can't think of how I can do this. How is this stable?
